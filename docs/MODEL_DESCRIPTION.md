# Model Description

## Objective

The model compares three governance arrangements for access to a social program:
hierarchy (`H`), delegation (`D`), and adaptive governance.

## Agents and environment

- Households are distributed across territories.
- Each territory has a local office.
- Households differ in vulnerability, access cost, trust, information, and brokerage.
- Offices differ in effort, opportunism, territorial complexity, and hazard.

## Household behavior

Households decide whether to apply based on expected gain, trust, information,
access cost, network signals, and application inertia.

## Office behavior

Offices process applicants under capacity constraints, screening noise,
monitoring intensity, and capture risk.

## Governance arrangements

- Hierarchy: higher coordination costs, stronger monitoring, lower leakage.
- Delegation: lower fixed costs, weaker monitoring, higher leakage risk.
- Adaptive: offices periodically compare governance scores and may switch.

## Learning and coevolution

- Households update trust and information through outcomes and neighborhood effects.
- Offices update effort, opportunism, and service quality through performance.
- In the adaptive scenario, governance may switch subject to review intervals,
  hysteresis, and minimum tenure.

## Main indicators

- Access rate
- Exclusion error
- Inclusion error
- Average trust
- Average monitoring
- Average service quality
- Average congestion
- Territorial inequality
- Delegated share
